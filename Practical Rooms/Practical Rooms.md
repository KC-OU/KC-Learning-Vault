# Practical Rooms

Practical rooms in TryHackMe and HackTheBox are interactive, browser-based, or VPN-connected virtual labs used to learn and test cybersecurity skills on real-world scenarios. They often include a virtual machine (VM) and tasks that require exploiting vulnerabilities to "capture the flag" (find a hidden text file).

- **TryHackMe Rooms:** These are highly guided, beginner-friendly learning environments often organized into paths, such as "Pre-Security" or "Offensive Pentesting," where you learn a concept (e.g., [Nmap](https://tryhackme.com/room/furthernmap)) and immediately apply it.
- **HackTheBox (HTB) Machines:** These are more advanced, often unguided, standalone virtual machines that emulate corporate networks and require independent research.
- **Synonyms/Related Terms:** Labs, Challenges, Machines, Scenarios, CTF (Capture The Flag) rooms.

**Key Examples & Usage**

- **TryHackMe:** Examples include "Agent Sudo" (exploiting a server) and "Crack the Hash" (password cracking). They provide "Walkthrough" rooms (guided) and "CTF/Challenge" rooms (independent).
- **HackTheBox:** Known for "Machines" (e.g., easy to insane difficulty). They offer "Starting Point" for beginners, "ProLabs" for advanced scenarios, and "Sherlocks" for blue team analysis.
- **Usage:** Used to practice network scanning, web application exploitation, [Active Directory](https://tryhackme.com/room/winadbasics) abuse, and privilege escalation.]
